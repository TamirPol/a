BBQ Scraper

Safety and Tools

I have used multiple tools for the Barbeque Scraper. Which include the:

Band Saw, Oscillating Spindle Sander, Drill Spress.

Safety in all machines:

  • Eye protection must be used when operating machines,
  • Complete attention must be directed at the machines,
  • Long hair must be tied back,
  • Jewelry must be taken off when entering woodshop,
  • Only operate machines under the teacher's permission

Band Saw

A bandsaw is a power saw with long sharp blade consisting of many metal teeth. The band saw is mainly used in woodworking and metalworking. The purpose of a band saw is to cut out shapes in wood, plastic, or metal. One of its advantages over other saws is its ability to cut out irregular or curved sides and shapes.

When using the bandsaw it is important to keep your fingers at least 50mm away from the blade and stand in front of the blade. Adjust the top of the blade approximately 6 mm above the material to be cut and always cut on the waste side of the line. When removing small scrap pieces all use a wooden stick.

Disc Sander

Disk sanders are prefabricated disks of sand paper. The Disk Sander is used to smooth materials such as woods and plastics. It is also used to remove small amounts of waste material.

When using the disc sander remove all saw dust and only sand on dry wood. Also always sand on the rotating-down side surface of the sander and never sand if the abrasive paper is loose or torn. Keep your fingers at least four inches away from the sander and do not push the material too hard against the machine. The wood must be positioned against the table at all times when using the machine.

Drill Press

The drill press is a machine used for making holes in hard surfaces such as wood and plastic. The drill is held in a rotating spindle and is fed into the work piece.

When drilling deep holes withdraw the drill often to prevent burning and damaging to the drill. To prevent hand injuries, secure the work piece to the table. Always use scrap piece of wood under the work to prevent damage to the drill press table and always brush the table with a shop brush. Secure the drill bit in the chuck by placing the chuck key in one of the three holes and twisting firmly. For a better finished cut, locate the center of a hole to be drilled with an X.

Oscillating Spindle Sander

An oscillating spindle sander or OSS for short is used for sanding curves. An OSS is a machine which consists of a tabletop witch a sanding drum sticking out through a hole in the top of the table. The drum is covered with abrasive sleeve, while also rotating and moving up and down.

Never try to sand metal as it could start a fire and don't leave the machine unless it has come to a complete stop. When sanding, sand against the direction the spindle is spinning. Do not leave the machine until it has come to a complete stop.
